OSINT Design Directions

Design Direction Exploration

Six visual directions for the OSINT investigation workspace, all built on the warm dark theme with Source Serif 4 + Inter typography. Each explores a different balance between the Q&A panel and graph canvas — the core split-view decision.

Click any direction to see a full mockup with realistic content from Maria's corruption investigation.

A: Balanced Workspace

35/65 split — recommended

Classic split view with Q&A panel left (35%), graph right (65%). Status bar at bottom. Entity detail cards overlay the graph. The default layout from our design discussions.

B: Prose-Forward

40/60 split — wider Q&A

Wider Q&A panel with generous typography. Suggested follow-up questions after each answer. Optimized for the reading experience — answers feel like a research brief.

C: Graph-Dominant

Full-screen graph + floating Q&A

Graph takes the full screen. Q&A appears as a floating command palette at the bottom. Answers float above the graph. Maximum canvas for visual exploration.

D: Focused Investigation

Icon sidebar + split view

Minimal 56px icon sidebar for navigation. Split workspace with compact Q&A panel. Maximum screen real estate for content. Feels like a modern IDE.

E: Tabbed Workspace

Tab navigation + split view

Tab bar for switching between investigation views (Investigate, Documents, Entities, Graph). Split view within the Investigate tab. Clear information hierarchy.

F: Narrative Drawer

Full graph + bottom drawer

Graph fills the workspace. Q&A lives in a pull-up drawer from the bottom — like a mobile sheet pattern adapted for desktop. Answers expand the drawer, collapse when exploring the graph.

How is Deputy Mayor Horvat connected to GreenBuild LLC?
Deputy Mayor Horvat is connected to GreenBuild LLC through two distinct paths that suggest a potential conflict of interest.

First, Horvat personally signed contract award #2024-089, which was awarded to GreenBuild LLC for the municipal parking structure project[1]. The contract value was 2.3 million EUR.

Second, and more significantly, Horvat's brother-in-law Marko Petrovic serves as registered director of Cascade Holdings[2], a holding company that owns 60% of GreenBuild LLC[3]. This ownership structure was registered in 2021, three years before the contract award.
